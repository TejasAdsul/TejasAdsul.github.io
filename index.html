Key Projects
Ant-Inspired Strategies for Innovation Processes:
As a part of my research thesis, I developed a novel bio-inspired model for finding efficient innovation discovery strategies in a scientific field. I analyzed 26000 articles in the field of Robotics from 1991-2020, performed Natural Language Processing on the article keywords and constructed a keyword co-occurrence network with 1600 nodes and 85000 links. This formed the environment of an agent-network model, with ants acting as the agent and interacting with the keyword network. I was able to extract novelty-boosting research strategies such as effective innovation dissemination, publishing failures, balancing incremental and breakthrough innovation, and a collaboration among researchers with different preferences regarding the choice of a project to pursue.

Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning Ant Model:
We conceived multi-agent reinforcement learning algorithms for efficient strategies in an ant foraging model. The environment consisted of food particles of different sizes and obstacles that ants had to navigate through. I worked on designing an algorithm for Joint Q-Learning and Decentralized Q-Learning. I also worked on implementing a Deep Q-Learning method with neural networks for robustness. I was able to train the ants to learn obstacle positions, find food and return to the nest as quickly as possible within 3 epochs for Joint Q-Learning and 20 epochs for Decentralized Q-Learning.

MIT Indoor Scenes Classification:
I built a Deep Learning Classifier for images of indoor scenes within an MIT dataset provided here. The dataset consists of 67 classes and a total of 15620 images, with at least 100 images in each class. I used the albumentations library for image augmentation to improve robustness. I used the ResNet50 Convolutional Neural Network with a modified FC-layer for the classifier. I also utilized cross entropy loss minimization and a learning rate scheduler for optimizing the training process. I also improved the speed by using GPU-acceleration. I was able to achieve an accuracy of 65%.

Computational Modeling and Optimization of Slithering Snake
University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign

We modeled the underlying physics, and simulated and visualized the optimized motion of a slithering snake in Python. I coupled the differential equations governing the physics of the snake, and performed time and spatial discretization to solve for dynamics and kinematics of the snake. I coded and implemented Genetic Algorithm and Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy to maximize the forward velocity of the snake by optimizing activation torque generated by the snake's muscles and analyzed numerous multi-minima functions like Griewank, Bohachevsky, Rastrigin.

Data Analysis of Nanoscale Thermal Protrusions on Hard-Disk Drives
Summer Intern
Computer Mechanics Laboratory, University of California at Berkeley

I conducted experiments on the nanoscale protrusions (~10-50 nm) of Thermal Fly-height Control Resistor (used to decrease fly-height of the head over the disk & also reduce the risk of head - disk contacts) using Zygo Interferometer. I used MATLAB to clean and process the data (captured from the experiments) by performing operations like transformations and noise filtering. I analyzed the data and derived a linear relation between the protrusions and power input to the resistor. I also worked on experimentally determining the thermal protrusions (~5 nm) of the Touchdown Sensor (used in the touchdown detection of the read/write head and the disk) using Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM) and Gwyddion software. Finally, I compared the precision and accuracy of Atomic Force Microscopy & Zygo Interferometer by obtaining and analyzing surface roughness parameters like arithmetic mean deviation, root mean squared, kurtosis, etc. of a hard disk drive.

Model Optimization of Additively Manufactured Hybrid Heat Exchanger

AHX-PI Lab, University of Maryland at College Park
