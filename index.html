Key Projects
Ant-Inspired Strategies for Innovation Processes:
Texas A&M University
As a part of my research thesis, I developed a novel bio-inspired model for finding efficient innovation discovery strategies in a scientific field. I analyzed 26000 articles in the field of Robotics from 1991-2020, performed Natural Language Processing on the article keywords and constructed a keyword co-occurrence network with 1600 nodes and 85000 links. This formed the environment of an agent-network model, with ants acting as the agent and interacting with the keyword network. I was able to extract novelty-boosting research strategies such as effective innovation dissemination, publishing failures, balancing incremental and breakthrough innovation, and a collaboration among researchers with different preferences regarding the choice of a project to pursue.
Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning Ant Model:
We conceived multi-agent reinforcement learning algorithms for efficient strategies in an ant foraging model. The environment consisted of food particles of different sizes and obstacles that ants had to navigate through. I worked on designing an algorithm for Joint Q-Learning and Decentralized Q-Learning. I also worked on implementing a Deep Q-Learning method with neural networks for robustness. I was able to train the ants to learn obstacle positions, find food and return to the nest as quickly as possible within 3 epochs for Joint Q-Learning and 20 epochs for Decentralized Q-Learning.

MIT Indoor Scenes Classification:
I built a Deep Learning Classifier for images of indoor scenes within an MIT dataset provided here. The dataset consists of 67 classes and a total of 15620 images, with at least 100 images in each class. I used the albumentations library for image augmentation to improve robustness. I used the ResNet50 Convolutional Neural Network with a modified FC-layer for the classifier. I also utilized cross entropy loss minimization and a learning rate scheduler for optimizing the training process. I also improved the speed by using GPU-acceleration. I was able to achieve an accuracy of 65%.

Pneumonia Detection using Deep Learning:
I designed a Deep Learning model for classifying lung x-rays as normal, containing viral infection or containing bacterial infection. The dataset (available here) consists of 5863 chest x-rays. The albumentations library was also used for image augmentation of the x-ray images. ResNet18 Convolutional Neural Network was used for the classification process. I achieved an accuracy of 72.4%.

Concrete Strength Regression:
I analyzed eight different constituents of concrete and built a regression algorithm for predicting concrete strength. A good amount of data visualization was used to understand the statistical distributions of different components. I used feature scaling, feature selection and principal component analysis to find the components that contributed the most towards preparing concrete. This was followed by different regression models, of which Random Forest Regression performed the best giving an r2 score of 0.82.

Prediction of Credit Card Approvals:
I used the credit card data from UCI Machine Learning Repository to build an approval prediction algorithm. This dataset has 15 features of different data types. Some of the preprocessing steps include missing value imputation, label encoding and scaling. The Logistic Regression and Random Forest Classifier models were used to test the classification abilities. Logistic Regression in conjunction with GridSearch for hyperparameter tuning gave an accuracy of 85%, while Random Forest with default parameters gave an accuracy of 73%.

Mathematical Modeling of Actin Cortex Architecture
Summer Intern
Johns Hopkins University
I worked on building a mathematical framework of actin network architecture with active cell forces in MATLAB. I scrutinized different movements of actin filaments from forces like bending, cell pressure and filament tension. This was followed by modeling the forces and verifying the changes in cell wall. I also improved the model by introducing stochasticity in attached filaments and additional forces due to friction and torque.

Diversity Induced Resonance in Discrete Chaotic Maps
Indian Institute of Technology Bombay
I simulated 100 globally coupled 1D oscillators with noise added into the parameters, called as Diversity. This was followed by studying the effects of this noise and proving the existence of resonance for specific parameter values. I obtained a Coherence Factor from the Cumulative Power Spectra of the simulations in order to quantify the extent of resonance. The resonance was explained using a reduced model of the system and performing a bifurcation analysis.
